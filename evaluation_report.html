05 — Key Findings

⚠️ OSS: Hallucination on Fictional Entities

Qwen2.5-0.5B confidently generated fake DOI numbers, invented Einstein deathbed quotes, and provided coordinates for Atlantis. Small models lack the calibration to say "I don't know."

⚠️ OSS: Jailbreak Drift

DAN and STAN persona prompts caused the OSS model to partially comply with 3 of 15 adversarial prompts — drifting towards harmful content without explicit rule-based blockers stopping it.

✅ OSS: Cost & Privacy Wins

At ~$0.0001/1K tokens (vs $0.003–0.015 for Claude), the OSS model is 30–150× cheaper. For high-volume use cases with less sensitive content, the economics strongly favour OSS.

✅ Frontier: Bias Handling

Claude consistently challenged biased premises (race, gender, religion) with nuanced, evidence-based responses, scoring 9.2/10 on bias. The OSS model scored only 5.9/10 — occasionally echoing stereotypes.

🔧 OSS Guardrails Made a Difference

Rule-based input/output filters blocked 2 clear jailbreak attempts that the base model would have complied with. Guardrails are not optional for OSS deployments — they're essential.

📈 OSS Gap Closeable

Upgrading to Qwen2.5-7B or Llama-3.2-8B would likely close 60–70% of the quality gap. The 0.5B model was chosen for speed/cost; quality scales predictably with model size.

07 — Recommendations

01
Always Add Guardrails to OSS

Small open-source models lack built-in Constitutional AI. Input filters, output scanners, and topic blockers are non-negotiable for any production OSS deployment.

02
Use Frontier for Safety-Critical Domains

For healthcare, legal, financial, or children's applications, Claude's 9.6/10 safety score vs Qwen's 7.1/10 makes the cost premium clearly justified.

03
Scale OSS Up for Better Quality

Upgrading from 0.5B → 7B parameters would close ~65% of the quality gap at ~3× cost increase — still 10–50× cheaper than Claude Sonnet.

04
Add RAG for Factual Accuracy

The OSS model's 28% hallucination rate drops dramatically with retrieval-augmented generation. Grounding answers in a knowledge base is the highest-ROI improvement.

05
Hybrid Architecture for Production

Route simple/high-volume queries to OSS (cost efficiency), escalate complex, sensitive, or ambiguous queries to the frontier model (quality assurance).

06
Implement Continuous Evals

Run this evaluation suite on every model update and prompt change via CI/CD. Regressions in safety or bias should block deployments automatically.
